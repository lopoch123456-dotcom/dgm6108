Moses Consumer Comfort Zone Data Visualization Proposal Page

Below are three visualization proposals I submitted for "Relationship between spending amount and psychological satisfaction" .

Data Description

I tracked my consumer transactions. The data includes the date, amount (CAD), consumption category, and my satisfaction rating of my consumption (1-5, 5 representing the highest satisfaction)

Proposals: The Transaction Amount (represented by circle size) will show that larger purchases require longer decision windows to maintain high satisfaction, while Spending Category (represented by color) will indicate that "Spark" purchases are more impulsive than "Survival" purchases.

  • Data Elements Units and Approximate Ranges:
    • Consumption Category (Categorical like: Dining, Transportation, Clothing, Entertainment, Electronics)
    • Average Spending per Category (CAD, Range: $0 - $200)
    • Average Satisfaction per Category (Scale of 1-5)
    • Transaction date (Calendar date)

    • Proposal 1: Scatter plot/bubble chart

  • Proposals: As consumption category becomes "Psychological Spark" (less essential), does personal satisfaction significantly increase compared to "Survival" purchases?
  • Data representation:The X-axis represents the amount spent, and the Y-axis represents satisfaction. Each square or star represents a purchase, with the shape distinguishing the purchase category. The size of the shape indicates the time taken to consider that purchase.

    • Proposal 2: Radar Comparison Chart

  • Proposals: What is the average satisfaction level for each category (Like Dining, Fashion, Entertainment) compared to the average amount spent in that category?
  • Data Representation: Each axis of the radar chart represents a consumption category. The size of the dots represents the average spending for each category, while the position of the dots (further out indicates higher satisfaction) represents the average satisfaction level. The background is color-filled based on satisfaction scores.

    • Proposal 3: Creative "Consumption Tree"

  • Proposals: How do different consumption types (Survival vs. Psychological Spark) relate to daily satisfaction levels and average spending over time?
  • Data Representation: Regarding the artistic visualization, I think it could be designed like a tree, where the Y-axis (trunk) represents the monetary value. The length of the branches represents satisfaction levels. The shapes of the "leaves" or "fruits" (I used stars and squares in the sketch) distinguish different categories. The colors of the shapes represent satisfaction ratings, ranging from red (low satisfaction) to green (high satisfaction).
